# Product Thesis: Compass

## The Problem

B2B SaaS companies are under immense pressure to adopt AI. Every board, every investor, every competitor is demanding AI strategy. The result is a predictable pattern of failure:

1. **Technology-first thinking**: Companies start with "which AI model should we use?" instead of "where does our organization actually need leverage?"
2. **Copycat deployment**: Teams replicate well-known AI use cases (chatbots, knowledge retrieval, summarization) without understanding whether those use cases solve their specific operational friction.
3. **Vendor capture**: Implementation platforms (Major, Bland, OpenAI, LangChain, etc.) optimize for their own adoption, not for the customer's highest-value opportunity.
4. **False precision**: ROI projections are fabricated or extrapolated from generic industry benchmarks that don't reflect the company's actual workflows, data quality, or organizational readiness.

The consequence: wasted AI budgets, organizational fatigue, and the eventual conclusion that "AI didn't deliver."

## The Insight

The question "which AI should we build?" is premature. The prior question is:

> **Where does this specific organization experience the most measurable operational leverage?**

Compass answers this question.

Compass is predicated on a single insight: **organizational workflows reveal AI opportunity before technical feasibility does**. A company's operational friction — the bottlenecks, handoffs, approvals, rework, waiting periods, and information gaps embedded in daily work — is the signal that tells you where AI creates leverage.

## What Compass Is

Compass is an **AI opportunity-discovery platform**. It:

- Takes a company profile (name, website URL, industry vertical)
- Researches the company publicly to infer operational structure, tech stack, and organizational maturity
- Runs an adaptive assessment that probes known friction points
- Pattern-matches against a library of 50 validated B2B SaaS workflow blueprints
- Applies an 8-stage reasoning pipeline to generate ranked AI Opportunity Maps
- Produces implementation blueprints with dependency sequencing and confidence scoring

Compass **determines where AI creates measurable leverage before implementation begins**.

## What Compass Is Not

Compass is not an AI implementation platform. It does not:

- Build, deploy, or host AI features
- Fine-tune or train models
- Recommend specific AI vendors, LLMs, or cloud providers
- Generate ROI numbers or payback periods
- Write code or integrate with existing systems
- Replace existing AI assessment or consulting work

Compass occupies the layer **above** implementation. Implementation platforms address the question: "Given we know what to build, how do we build it?" Compass addresses the prior question: "What should we build?"

This positioning is intentional. Compass partners with implementation platforms rather than competing with them.

## The Market Gap

The current market for AI adoption has a missing layer:

| Layer | Question | Who Occupies It |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| **Strategy** | What business should we be in? | Management consultants |
| **Opportunity Discovery** | Where does AI create leverage for us? | **COMPASS — currently empty** |
| **Implementation Planning** | How do we build this capability? | AI consultancies, system integrators |
| **Platform/Infrastructure** | How do we run this AI? | Major, Bland, OpenAI, LangChain, AWS |

The gap is conspicuous. Every other layer has established players. Opportunity discovery is either skipped entirely (companies jump from strategy to implementation) or handled generically via frameworks that don't account for organizational specificity.

## Initial Vertical: B2B SaaS

Compass launches in **B2B SaaS** for three reasons:

1. **Homogeneous operating model**: B2B SaaS companies share remarkably similar departmental structures (Sales, Marketing, CS, Support, Finance, Product, Engineering, People/HR, Legal, Operations). This makes blueprint transferability high.
2. **Data-rich signals**: B2B SaaS companies generate abundant public signals — job postings, product changelogs, funding announcements, customer reviews, leadership interviews, engineering blogs — that reveal operational structure and friction.
3. **Immediate AI applicability**: The workflows of B2B SaaS are predominantly information work — writing, reviewing, routing, responding, qualifying, reporting — which is precisely where current AI capabilities create the most leverage.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over assertion**: Every opportunity must cite evidence, classified as [User] (from assessment responses), [Research] (from public data), or [Inference] (derived from pattern matching).

2. **Confidence transparency**: Every recommendation includes a confidence level (Confirmed, High, Medium, Low) with the reasoning behind that rating.

3. **No fabricated precision**: Compass does not produce ROI numbers, percentage improvements, or dollar projections. These are inherently speculative and undermine credibility.

4. **Organizational specificity**: Generic AI recommendations ("implement a chatbot") are not permitted. Every opportunity must be grounded in the specific organization's workflows, systems, and constraints.

5. **Dependency-aware sequencing**: Recommendations include implementation dependencies. Compass identifies which opportunities are prerequisites for others and which can proceed independently.

6. **Adaptive assessment**: No two companies receive the same assessment. The question engine adapts based on company profile, industry, and prior responses.

7. **Knowledge graph enrichment**: Every interaction enriches an organizational knowledge graph that improves subsequent reasoning.

## Competitive Differentiation

Compass competes with:
- **Internal strategy exercises**: Most companies attempt their own AI opportunity assessment via spreadsheets and whiteboards. These lack rigor, evidence, and blueprint libraries.
- **Consulting engagements**: McKinsey, BCG, and boutique AI consultancies offer similar services but at 10-100x the cost and 3-6 month timelines.
- **Vendor assessments**: AI vendors offer "readiness assessments" that are thinly veiled sales motions. They always conclude you need their product.
- **AI workshops**: Facilitated sessions that generate ideas but not evidence-grounded recommendations.

Compass's advantage is **systematic, evidence-based, organization-specific opportunity discovery delivered at software speed and software price**.

## Future Verticals

While initial focus is B2B SaaS, the framework is vertical-agnostic. Future expansions include:
- Professional services (legal, accounting, consulting)
- Healthcare operations (not clinical — administrative workflows)
- Financial services (compliance, reporting, client servicing)
- Manufacturing (supply chain, quality, documentation)
- Education (administration, curriculum development, student services)

Each vertical requires a new blueprint library, but the reasoning engine, assessment framework, and intelligence layer transfer directly.
